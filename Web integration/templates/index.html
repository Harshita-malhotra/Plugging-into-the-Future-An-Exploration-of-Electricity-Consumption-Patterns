Electricity Consumption Analysis in India

Electricity is a vital driver of economic activity and reflects both industrial productivity and household energy access. This project analyzes electricity consumption patterns across Indian states from January 2019 to December 2020, including the impact of the COVID-19 lockdown.

Project Overview

In 2015–16, agriculture accounted for the highest share (17.89%) of electricity consumption in India. Despite relatively low electricity tariffs, India's per capita electricity consumption remains below global averages, indicating growth potential and infrastructural challenges.

The COVID-19 pandemic introduced major changes in energy demand across sectors. Industrial consumption declined while residential demand increased during lockdown.

Scenario 1 – Change in Overall Consumption Trends

Tracking electricity consumption from January 2019 to December 2020 highlights how seasonal demand, economic changes, and the nationwide lockdown influenced energy usage.

Scenario 2 – Regional Variations in Demand

Electricity usage varies across Northern, Southern, Eastern, Western, and Northeastern regions due to climate, industrial activity, and population density.

Scenario 3 – Recovery After Lockdown

After lockdown restrictions were lifted, electricity demand began recovering across states, though the pace varied depending on economic activity.
